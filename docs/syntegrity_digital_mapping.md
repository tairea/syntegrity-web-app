# Team Syntegrity: Process Stages & Digital Interface Mapping

A stage-by-stage mapping of Stafford Beer's Team Syntegrity protocol to a digital platform design.

## Process & Interface Table

| # | Stage | What happens in the original protocol | Digital interface / application |
|---|---|---|---|
| **0** | **Pre-event setup** | Problem owner works with lead facilitator to draft the opening question. Participants (typically 30) are selected to represent the diversity of perspectives relevant to the issue. Venue, schedule, and documentation protocols are agreed. | **Sponsor dashboard:** guided wizard to draft the opening question with examples of good questions, invite the 30 participants, set the schedule, configure documentation/privacy settings. **Participant onboarding:** short tutorial explaining the icosahedron metaphor, four roles each person plays, and what to expect. Rotating 3D icosahedron visual. Profile pages capturing each participant's perspective/expertise. |
| **1** | **Opening session** | Participants arrive, receive a short briefing, are invited to set aside inhibitions and begin generating statements. | **Live kickoff room:** video plenary with the opening question pinned. Brief interactive walkthrough of the rules. Icebreaker that doubles as a check that everyone's audio/video works. |
| **2a** | **Problem Jostle — statement generation** | Participants write any statement they feel is important on post-it notes. Only rule: someone in the group should be likely to disagree with it. Groups typically generate 100–200 statements. | **Sticky-note canvas:** infinite-pan board where participants drop short statements. Opening question pinned at top. Author shown (or anonymous, depending on settings). **Validity nudge:** soft prompt before submission asks "Would someone in this group likely disagree with this?" — guidance, not a block. **Async mode option:** stage can run over 24–48 hours rather than a single session. Live activity feed. |
| **2b** | **Problem Jostle — clustering** | Statements addressing similar points are clustered. Participants gather around flip charts to discuss themes. Issues generating most debate are restated as "Aggregated Statements of Importance." | **Live clustering interface:** participants drag related notes together. AI assist suggests clusters based on semantic similarity; humans always confirm. Clusters get working titles anyone can edit. Discussion threads attach to each cluster. |
| **2c** | **Problem Jostle — aggregation & nomination** | Five signatures required to nominate a statement for the next stage. Signers don't need to agree — only believe it should be discussed. 15–50 candidate statements typically posted (~25 norm). | **Aggregated Statement editor:** each cluster becomes a candidate statement. Anyone can propose wording. Five digital co-signs promote it to candidate status. Signature sidebar shows who has endorsed what. **Merging interface:** facilitators (or group via consensus) can elide near-duplicates with side-by-side merge view. |
| **3** | **Selecting 12 topics** | Facilitator asks group to merge similar topics. Participants distribute 20 sticker votes across remaining candidates (allowing weighting). Vote counted; clear winners and losers emerge. If choices remain, a final vote decides. | **Sticker voting screen:** each participant gets 20 stickers to distribute across candidate topics. Live tallies (or hidden until close — configurable). Drag multiple stickers onto a topic you feel strongly about. **Topic confirmation view:** 12 winners appear with vote distribution; "maybe" topics flagged for facilitator-led merging discussion. |
| **4** | **Role assignment** | Participants rank their preferred topics 1–12. Hancock's algorithm shuffles topic placements on the icosahedron and participants' preferences to optimise satisfaction. Each person assigned to one of 30 struts with two colours; gets 2 participant roles + 2 critic roles. | **Topic ranking screen:** drag-and-drop the 12 topics into preferred order. Short blurb on each topic. **Allocation algorithm** runs in background — digital descendant of Hancock's program. **Personal role reveal:** animated 3D icosahedron lights up your strut, your two participant-teams, your two critic-teams, and colour-coding. **My Schedule view:** clear timetable showing which sessions you're in, in what role. |
| **5a** | **Outcome Resolve — Iteration 1** | Each team meets for the first time. 5 participants drive discussion, 5 critics listen for most of it. Facilitator captures key points. Critics get a designated speaking window near the end. Team produces a written statement. Discussion is exploratory, opening up the territory. | **Synchronous team room** (live mode): video + chat. 5 participants visible, 5 critics in designated panel — muted during participant discussion, unmuted for critique window. Live shared document. **Speaking-time meter** ensures equal voice. **Critic timer** shows when the critic window opens. **Statement composer** in final minutes — collaborative editor with version history. **Async mode** (for distributed events): dedicated team thread with designated participant posts, critic posts, and working statement document; time-boxed (e.g., 48 hours). |
| **5b** | **Between iterations** | Each team's statement gets posted somewhere visible to the whole infoset. Participants in off-slots browse other teams' statements and attach "graffiti" — applause, comments, questions. Mandatory breaks let ideas settle. | **Public statement wall:** single page showing all 12 teams' current statements side by side, updated live. Browsable, persistent. Lightweight reactions (the digital "graffiti"): emoji applause, threaded micro-comments, question flags. **Reading-tracker** shows participants which statements they've reviewed before the next iteration begins. |
| **5c** | **Outcome Resolve — Iteration 2** | Teams meet again. Participants arrive having sat in other team meetings since iteration 1 — they unconsciously carry ideas across. Statements get sharper. Cross-references to other teams' work appear. | Same team room infrastructure as 5a, but session begins with a **"what's reverberating"** prompt — the facilitator or AI assist surfaces 2–3 themes from other teams' statements that might bear on this team's topic. Statement editor pre-loads the team's iteration-1 statement for revision rather than starting blank. |
| **5d** | **Outcome Resolve — Iteration 3** | Final round of team meetings. Participants have read all iteration-2 statements on the wall. Discussion shifts from generating content to integrating and articulating — making the final statement say exactly what the team means. | Same team room infrastructure, with the **public wall accessible as a side panel** during the meeting. Statement composer now in "refinement mode" — track changes visible, with the iteration-1 and iteration-2 versions browsable for comparison. A **convergence check** prompt at the end: "Does this statement reflect what the team agrees on, including agreement to disagree?" |
| **6** | **Closing plenary** | All 12 teams come together. Each presents its final statement. The whole group sees how the pieces fit together. Often a high point of the event. | **Final statements gallery:** all 12 outcome statements arranged around the icosahedron visualisation. Click any vertex to read the full statement and its evolution across iterations. **Reverberation map:** visualisation showing how ideas migrated between teams across iterations — making the "pollen" metaphor tangible. **Live presentation room:** each team has a slot to present; rest of infoset watches and reacts. |
| **7** | **Face Planning / follow-up** (optional but common) | Joe Truss's protocol: groups of 3, corresponding to triangular faces of the icosahedron, meet to plan actions advancing the themes of their three topics. 20 triangular faces yield 20 integrated action plans. Can fit into three time blocks at end of event. | **Triangle assignments:** platform auto-schedules 20 face meetings, each with 3 participants linked to the three topics whose vertices form that triangle. **Action plan templates:** structured documents linking each action to its three source topics. **Commitment tracker:** owners, deadlines, dependencies — handed off to the sponsoring organisation's project tool of choice. |
| **8** | **Documentation & archive** | Participants given print-outs of Statements of Importance, Aggregated Statements, final twelve topics, and Outcome Resolve statements. Some events documented with video or CD-ROM. | **Event archive:** auto-generated record containing the opening question, all original statements, clustering history, voting results, role assignments, all three iterations of each team's statement, graffiti, final plenary, and Face Planning outputs. Downloadable as a structured bundle; selected sections shareable. Privacy controls applied per the event's documentation protocol. |

## Cross-cutting Design Considerations

These apply across multiple stages and don't fit cleanly into a single row:

- **Persistent icosahedron navigation** in a corner of the platform throughout — showing where you are, what teams you're in, and how the conversation is connecting.
- **Privacy modes** configurable per event — full attribution vs unattributed contributions for sensitive topics.
- **Equal-voice guardrails** baked into the UI rather than relying entirely on facilitators — speaking timers, contribution counters, gentle nudges.
- **Reduced-size variants** supported as alternative templates (octahedron for ~12 people, tetrahedron for 6, etc.).
- **AI assist** used carefully — semantic clustering, scribing, summarising — but never for choosing topics, adjudicating content, or playing critic.
- **Online format options** for groups that can't commit to multi-day in-person events: compressed sprints, drip-feed over two weeks, hybrid (one live day + async tail), or three evenings on consecutive days. The three-iteration rhythm of the Outcome Resolve must be preserved in any variant.
